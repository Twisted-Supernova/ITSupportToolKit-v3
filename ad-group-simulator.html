About AD Group Nesting

Group Nesting: In Active Directory, groups can be members of other groups, creating a hierarchy.

Effective Membership: A user's effective group membership includes:

  • Direct group memberships (user directly added to group)
  • Indirect memberships (through nested groups)

Example: If User A is in Group B, and Group B is in Group C, then User A effectively belongs to both Group B and Group C.

Use Cases:

  • Understanding permission inheritance
  • Troubleshooting access issues
  • Planning group structure
  • Auditing effective permissions
